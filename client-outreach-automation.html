Implementation Process & Project Management

Phase 1: Discovery & Planning (2 weeks)

  • Stakeholder Interviews: Understanding current manual processes and pain points
  • Data Analysis: Client database audit and service pattern identification
  • Requirements Gathering: Defining automation scope and success criteria
  • Workflow Design: Mapping optimal re-targeting logic and sequences

Phase 2: Development (6 weeks)

  • Prototype Development: Initial workflow creation in Power Automate
  • Testing Cycles: Small-scale testing with pilot client segments
  • Refinement: Iterative improvements based on test results
  • Integration: Full Excel database integration and validation

Phase 3: Deployment (2 weeks)

  • Full-Scale Launch: Deployment across all 5,000+ client records
  • Team Training: Staff training on monitoring and managing workflows
  • Performance Monitoring: Real-time tracking of system performance
  • Quick Adjustments: Rapid response to initial deployment issues

Phase 4: Optimization (Ongoing)

Project Management Insights & Lessons Learned

Critical Success Factors

Agile Methodology: Using agile project management with 2-week sprints enabled rapid prototyping and iterative refinement, ensuring the solution met real-world needs.

Data Quality Focus: Investing time upfront in cleaning and organizing the client database was crucial for accurate targeting and preventing errors in automated outreach.

User-Centered Design: Involving the team early in workflow design ensured the automated system complemented rather than disrupted existing processes.

Change Management: Comprehensive training and gradual rollout helped team members transition from manual to automated processes with minimal resistance.

Metrics-Driven Optimization: Establishing clear KPIs from day one (retention rates, time savings, engagement metrics) enabled data-driven improvements and ROI demonstration.

Scalability Planning: Building the workflow with scalability in mind from the start allowed for easy expansion to additional client segments without redesign.
